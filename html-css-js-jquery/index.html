My Tech Journey

  • 2010-2013

    The Curious Gamer

    My first experience with programming occured back in elementary school. I was an avid gamer and playing the popular MMORPG Runescape at the time, the game fascinated me to no end and I always wondered how it was made. My curiosity drove me to research my questions and I found out that the company that made it, Jagex, made a framework called Runescript out of the well-known programming language Java to build out the game. I ended up studying both Java and Runescript in order to gain the skillset neccassary for building out my own renditions of the game, as well as many other games I was interested in at the time.

  • 2013-2015

    A C++ Programmer is Born

    Soon after my experiences with Java, I moved over to C++ and pursued a more textbook approach to learning code. Instead of basing my study on ideas and games that I wanted to create, I took the initiative to thoroughly learn C++ from scratch. I learned about what object oriented programming was (by definition), along with a lot of other terminology to really familiarize myself with the programming world. I also tested my knowledge by recreating certain projects through youtube tutorials and my own knowledge. Examples include classics such as Snake, Pong and eventually even Chess as a part of a University course I took.

  • 2015-2019

    Entering the Workforce

    During this time I was coding more as a hobby rather than for educational purposes for the majority of it, save for courses in school which I felt hardly improved my existing skillset. I ventured into Swift for iOS dev, namely to recreate flappy bird and test out some other game ideas I had. The main takeaway from this time period was that I worked my first job in the field as a designer and developer, through which I was able to test my dev skills.

  • 2019-2020

    Expanding My Horizons

    I finally came to the conclusion that I want to pursue Software Engineering and related aspects of the programming world with everything that I've learned. And due to imposter syndrome, as many of us have, I've been focusing on improving myself first by building out multiple good projects with modern tech stacks. I can confidentally say that I've thoroughly learned MERN and Python at this point, but I'd argue it was at a hinderance to my skills from the past such as my C++ and Java which I feel have decayed a bit. My curious and open-minded mindset still persists to this day, I'm still always looking up the best ways to create ideas I have and it has lead me to some fun experiences with Unity/C#, Go and more.
